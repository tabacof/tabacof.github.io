Summary


I am studying for a master's degree in machine learning. I work with variational autoencoders, adversarial image generation and Bayesian neural networks. I study at Unicamp, considered to be the top Brazilian electrical engineering graduate program by CAPES, a federal agency. Unicamp is a research university and is ranked second best in Latin America according to the Time Higher Education.

My graduate work so far has led to a paper that is to be published in an IEEE conference (IJCNN 2016). The conference will take place in Vancouver, Canada, by the end of July. The preprint has already received three citations and two media stories (see publications for more details).

I have professional experience as the first R&D engineer in I.Systems, an industrial automation startup company. We develop advanced controllers and optimizers for the process industries using Fuzzy and statistical process control. I.Systems now has over 20 employers and has been invested by a venture capital fund. Besides creating new and improving core system modules, I have participated to deployments on a diverse array of industries (Lanxess AG, CBA, Votorantim, etc). I have managed three research interns, and I will lead two engineers working on a state sponsored research grant, starting mid-2016.

I graduated with distinction in computer engineering at Unicamp, and I received a certificate of studies on Automation and Intelligent Systems for graduate work done while an undergraduate.

I have extensive international experience (USA, Canada and Czech Republic), and I am fluent in English and Portuguese. I am interested in working abroad (available for relocation).
